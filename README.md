# HR Analytics Dashboard – Project Overview

## Project Title
Chocolate Company HR Dashboard

## Objective
To analyze employee data and provide insights into workforce demographics, organizational structure, compensation, and employee behavior to support data-driven HR decisions.

## Key Metrics Tracked
- Total number of employees
- Attrition rate
- Average sick leave hours
- Average vacation hours
- Number of candidates

## Analysis Areas
The dashboard explores:
- Employee distribution by age, gender, and marital status
- Workforce distribution by organization level and tenure
- Salary comparison across departments
- Employee rate trends

## Tools Used
- Power BI for visualization and dashboard creation
- SQL to analyze HR dataset containing employee records

## Outcome
The dashboard provides a clear visual summary of workforce composition, retention, and compensation patterns, enabling quick insights for HR planning and decision-making.

## Sumary of Finding
Number of Employees: The company has 290 employees.

- Attrition Rate: Employee attrition is 1.72%, indicating low staff turnover.
- Average Sick Leave Hours: Employees take an average of 45 sick leave hours.
- Average Vacation Hours: Employees take an average of 50 vacation hours.
- Number of Candidates: There are 13 candidates in the hiring pipeline.
- Employees by Age: Most employees are concentrated in the 30–45 age range.
- Employees by Gender: The workforce is majority female (206) compared to males (84).
- Employees by Marital Status: Employees are almost evenly split between married (146) and single (144).
- Employees by Tenure: Most employees have long tenure, peaking around 16 years.
- Rate by Number of Employees: The rate increases step-by-step to a final total, showing cumulative contribution.
- Employees by Organization Level: Level 4 has the most employees (191), while Level 1 has the fewest (6).
- Average Salary by Department: Research & Development has the highest average salary, while other departments are lower.
